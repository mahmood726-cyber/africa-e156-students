In the ecological analysis of clinical research systems, does the selection pressure of limited funding create a hardier research profile in Africa compared to Europe? This metadata audit applied natural selection models to two thousand trial records across four global regions to evaluate the relationship between initiation volume and completion success. Reviewers used a selection-pressure model and reported the hardy-hub index as the primary comparative estimand for institutional resilience and research efficiency. The primary result revealed that African research hubs function as hardy ecosystems where a high percentage of initiated trials survive to publish results despite severe resource constraints.
