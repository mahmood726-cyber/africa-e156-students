Full Text

In global cardiology, does interventional heart failure trial distribution reflect the distinct burden carried by Africa? This audit queried ClinicalTrials.gov for heart failure trials across Africa, the United States, India, and Brazil, plus peripartum cardiomyopathy, rheumatic heart disease, and endomyocardial fibrosis in Africa. Researchers classified each African trial by sponsor origin, phenotype, and intervention class, reporting the Condition Colonialism Index as primary estimand. Africa had 41 heart failure trials versus 1,855 in the United States, a 45-fold disparity despite carrying ten percent of global burden. SGLT2 inhibitor and device trials were near-absent, rheumatic heart disease had two trials despite 240,000 annual deaths preventable by two-cent penicillin, and peripartum cardiomyopathy trials were rare despite Nigeria having the highest incidence. These findings expose a fundamental evidence gap where therapies validated in elderly ischemic populations are extrapolated to younger non-ischemic African patients without confirmation. Interpretation is limited by reliance on one public registry which may undercount locally funded studies.
